03

Method — how we work · five field notes

How we work

We've watched good projects go sideways for the same few reasons. So we work the opposite way.

  1. № 01

    You talk to the people doing the work.

    No account manager relaying messages to someone you'll never meet. The person who builds it is the person you brief — and the person who answers when you call.

    — no middlemen
  2. № 02

    We say the price before we start.

    You get a clear scope and a number up front. If the work grows, we tell you before the bill does.

    — no surprise invoices
  3. № 03

    Small, finished pieces beat one big reveal.

    We ship in steps you can see and use, instead of vanishing for three months. You catch what's wrong early, while it's cheap to change.

    — nothing big-bang
  4. № 04

    Plain words, always.

    We'll explain what we're doing and why, without the jargon. If you can't tell a friend what you bought, we haven't done our job.

    — no jargon
  5. № 05

    We hand it over clean.

    Documented, tidy, and yours. You're never locked in or held hostage for a password. Good work should outlast the people who built it.

    — no lock-in

We're a small team on purpose. That means we say no to work we can't do well — and yes means yes.

05

About — small as an advantage

Avian is family-run.
That's not a detail — it's the whole point.

We started Avian because we'd seen too much technology sold by people who'd never have to live with it. The big agency takes the brief, ships the project, and moves on. The small business is left holding a system nobody will explain.

We wanted to be the other kind. So we kept the team small — close enough that we know your name, your setup, and what you're trying to get done. When you call, you reach us, not a ticket number.

Small isn't a limitation we're apologizing for. It's how the work stays careful. Fewer clients, more attention, our name on every piece of it. We answer for what we make, because there's no one else to point to — and we like it that way.

Family-run · independent

You'll always reach a person who already knows your setup.

Our rule: never ship something we wouldn't want to maintain ourselves.
